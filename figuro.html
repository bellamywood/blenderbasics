Camera controls

Zoom, orbit, pan
  • Mouse wheel = zoom
  • Right mouse drag = orbit (spin around)
  • Middle mouse drag = pan (slide view)
  • Shift + Right mouse drag = pan
  • Ctrl + Right mouse drag = zoom
Mini challenge:
Orbit around the centre, zoom in, then pan so you can still see your model.
Hint if you “lost” your model
  • Zoom out a lot, then orbit slowly.
  • If you selected something, try moving it slightly to see where it is.

Tools and modes

What you edit + how you move it
Remember:
Mode = what you’re editing • Tool = how you change it

Tools:

  • W Move
  • E Scale
  • R Rotate

Modes:

  • A Object mode (whole object)
  • S Face mode (polygons)
  • D Edge mode
  • F Vertex mode
Hint: quick fixes
  • Undo: Ctrl + Z
  • Duplicate: Space (clone)
  • Delete: Del or Backspace
  • Snap to grid while moving: hold X

4

Bowling pin: start shape

Create a cylinder and select the top face
  1. Press A for Object mode.
  2. Add a Cylinder (primitive shape).
  3. If you see “Sides/Segments”, set it to 8.
  4. Use W Move and E Scale to make it short and chunky.
  5. Press S for Face mode.
  6. Click the top face of the cylinder.
Hint: I can’t select faces

Press S (Face mode), then click a face. If it still won’t select, click the object first in Object mode.

◀ Back Next ▶
5

Bowling pin: shape it

Extrude → Scale (repeat)
The pattern:
T Extrude → E Scale → repeat
  1. Top face selected? Good. Press T (Extrude) and pull up a little.
  2. Press E (Scale) and make it slightly thinner.
  3. Repeat: extrude up → scale in → extrude up.
  4. For the head: extrude a bit, then scale slightly out to round it.
Hint: make it smoother
  • Subdivision level: press 4 or 5 (don’t go too high).
  • Smooth shading: press K.

6

Bowling ball

Create a sphere and place it
  1. Press A for Object mode.
  2. Add a Sphere.
  3. Use W Move to place it near the lane.
  4. Use E Scale to make it smaller than the pin base.
  5. Optional polish: press K for smooth shading.
Optional: finger holes
  1. Press S (Face mode).
  2. Hold Shift and click 3 faces.
  3. Press T (Extrude) and push in slightly.

7

Bowling lane

Make a long flat surface
  1. Press A (Object mode).
  2. Add a Plane (or Grid).
  3. Press E (Scale) and stretch it into a long lane.
  4. Use W (Move) to put it under your pin and ball.
Hint: keeping it tidy

While moving objects, hold X to snap to the grid.

8

Make 10 pins

Clone and arrange them
  1. Press A (Object mode).
  2. Click your pin.
  3. Press Space to clone (duplicate).
  4. Move the new pin into place.
  5. Repeat until you have 10 pins in a triangle at the end of the lane.
Triangle idea:
1 pin at the front, then 2 behind, then 3, then 4 (total 10).
Hint: my clone is on top of the old one

After pressing Space, move it immediately so you can see the copy.

9

Finish and keep your work

Make it look good and save/export

Final checklist:

  • Lane is visible and flat.
  • 10 pins are standing at the end.
  • Ball is near the lane.
Quick “looks better” keys:
Render mode: 123 • Smoothing: 4 or 5 • Smooth shading: K • Light: L

Keep your work:

  • If you can export: export as OBJ or STL.
  • If export is blocked: take a screenshot of your best view.
Extension (if you finish early)
  • Add gutter rails.
  • Add lane markings (thin rectangles).
  • Colour the ball and pins if materials/colours are available.
